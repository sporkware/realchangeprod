Advocacy

Fighting for economic, social and racial justice since 1994

We fight for what is morally right, not what is politically possible.

From our creation in 1994, Real Change's approach to combating homelessness and poverty meets direct, immediate needs while working toward long-term, systems-change. We challenge ongoing camp sweeps, fighting the criminalization of poverty and homelessness with community-led solutions that honor the autonomy and dignity of our unhoused neighbors.

Stay up to date with Real Change advocacy by signing up for our Action Alerts.

Vendor Organizing Committee

The Vendor Organizing Committee (VOC), a group of vendor activists, works alongside our team to identify policy goals and advocate for change.

The VOC helps select which policy decisions are most important and organizes actions around these decisions on the city, county and state level. VOC members take speaking training, work on writing testimony to share in city and county meetings, and most importantly bring their experience as neighbors living unsheltered to this work. It is central to this work for us to involve active Real Change vendors in the decision-making and that we remain accountable to the people most impacted by the advocacy work.

This is what we fight for:

  • Social Housing
  • Housing First
  • Stop The Sweeps!
  • Defunding SPD and Investing in Community
  • RV safelots and wraparound services for vehicle residents
  • Ending Evictions and Protecting Renters
  • Drug User Health
  • Harm Reduction
  • Rapid Expansion of Tiny House Villages, Hotel and Low Barrier 24/7 Shelter
  • Hygiene Services for All
